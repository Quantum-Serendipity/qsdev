# How to Launch a Dev Tool on Hacker News

- **Source**: https://www.markepear.dev/blog/dev-tool-hacker-news-launch
- **Retrieved**: 2026-05-15

---

## Official HN Admin Guidelines

Hacker News admins provide explicit launch guidance at `news.ycombinator.com/yli.html`. The core principles focus on authenticity and serving the community rather than selling.

### Tone & Style Requirements

Write in a personal voice rather than corporate speak. Address the HN audience as "fellow builders and engineers" you might discuss work with casually. Avoid superlatives like "fastest" or "best"—modest language proves more persuasive.

Critically, "Don't sell to this audience. If you try, they will close the tab. Instead, interest them, then let them sell themselves."

### Product & Offering Standards

Remove all barriers to trying your tool. Offer free access or, secondarily, demos or videos. Transparency matters: clearly explain your pricing model and revenue strategy. Structure your call-to-action to invite community feedback rather than drive conversions.

### Post Structure

Follow this sequence:
1. Introduce your team
2. State your product's function in one clear sentence
3. Explain the core problem and its significance
4. Share your personal backstory for building this
5. Detail your technical solution
6. Articulate what differentiates your approach technically
7. Invite community suggestions

### Engagement Expectations

Be prepared to respond quickly and comprehensively to comments. Ensure team members don't post artificial support comments. Engage substantively—the HN community values technical depth. When facing criticism, "Answer objections by first finding something to agree with, even if it's just the positive intention behind the comment."

## Additional Success Factors

**Positioning matters:** The audience strongly favors open-source or privacy-first products. Highlight "open-source alternative to X" directly in your title if applicable.

**Clarity is essential:** Make what you've built immediately obvious. A GitHub repository link signals legitimacy, accessibility, and actual functionality better than proprietary websites.

## Case Study: Fly.io's Success

The highest-upvoted dev tool launch—Fly.io's announcement—exemplifies these principles. The post introduced the team authentically, used descriptive language rather than marketing hyperbole, provided transparent pricing details, and fielded 53+ substantive comments from the founding team member within the thread.

## Common Pitfalls

Less successful launches often fail to clearly explain their purpose, use overstated claims, lack transparent pricing, or position themselves as commercial solutions first rather than community resources.
