# How to Engage Your Audience with Choose Your Own Adventure Presentations
- **Source**: https://depictdatastudio.com/how-to-engage-your-audience-with-choose-your-own-adventure-presentations/
- **Retrieved**: 2026-05-15

## Overview
This non-linear presentation method empowers audiences to vote on which topics the presenter covers, allowing them to direct the flow of the session rather than experiencing a predetermined sequence of slides.

## When to Use It

**Best for:**
- Topics where segments can be delivered in any order without sequential dependencies
- Presentations where audience interests vary significantly
- Sessions where presenter has deep expertise across multiple related areas

**Requirements for Success:**
The presenter must be "an expert in your topic area and have tons of presentation experience." Additionally, presenters need to be "nimble enough to speak about any of your topics in any order, and to adjust the time spent on each topic on the fly."

## Structure & Implementation

**Four-Step Process:**

1. **Display Table of Contents** - Show an overview slide listing all possible topics
2. **Gather Audience Votes** - Ask attendees to select their preferred topics (via chat, polls, etc.)
3. **Use Hyperlinks** - Click linked text/icons on the contents slide to jump to chosen segments
4. **Repeat** - Return to contents slide after each section; repeat for remaining topics

**Technical Setup:**
- Create hyperlinks in PowerPoint via Insert > Link
- Select "Place in this Document"
- Link to specific slide numbers for each topic
- Audience members can add invisible rectangle links to direct to case studies or optional content

## Benefits

- Puts "our audience in the driver's seat"
- Increases engagement beyond traditional Q&A sessions
- Demonstrates flexibility and responsiveness to audience needs
- Creates interactive, participatory experiences

## When to Avoid

This method fails when topics require sequential learning or have prerequisite knowledge dependencies where order matters for comprehension.
