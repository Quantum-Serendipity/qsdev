# Cyber Insurance Costs & Coverage Data

## Average Premium Costs

**2024 Baseline:** Businesses spent between $1,200-$7,000 annually, with a median of ~$2,000/year.

**Historical Reference:** A 2019 study found the average cost of a cyber liability policy in 2019 was $1,500 per year for $1 million in coverage, with a $10,000 deductible.

**Market Volatility:** Premiums rose nearly 80% in Q2 2022, then decreased 50-60% for some policyholders by 2024.

## Coverage Limits & Terms

Standard cyber liability coverage limits range from $500,000 to $5 million per occurrence. Deductibles are customizable; lower deductibles mean higher premiums but reduced out-of-pocket costs during claims.

## What Policies Cover (First-Party)

First-party coverage includes:
- Forensic analysis
- Public relations services
- Client/customer notifications
- Credit monitoring services
- Business income loss

## Factors Affecting Premium Costs

Seven key drivers:
1. **Company Size** — More employees increase phishing risk exposure
2. **Industry Type** — Tech and finance face higher rates than construction
3. **Data Volume/Sensitivity** — Healthcare and retail handling payment cards pay more
4. **Annual Revenue** — Higher revenue attracts cybercriminals
5. **Security Measures** — Strong security protocols lower premiums
6. **Policy Terms** — Higher coverage limits increase costs
7. **Claims History** — Previous claims increase premiums

## Cost-Benefit Data

A single ransomware attack averages $1.85 million in costs, making insurance substantially cost-effective for most businesses.
