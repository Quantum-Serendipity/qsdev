# Tips for Your First KubeCon Presentation - Part 1
- **Source**: https://kubernetes.io/blog/2018/10/18/tips-for-your-first-kubecon-presentation-part-1/
- **Retrieved**: 2026-05-15

## Understanding the Challenge

**Acceptance Rate & Competition**
- Only ~10-13% of submissions are accepted
- Over 1,000 submissions per KubeCon event
- Three KubeCon events annually (US, EU, Asia regions)

**Unique Presentation Pressures**
- All presentations are recorded, including the speaker on stage
- Audience includes Kubernetes experts, maintainers, and SIG leads
- Content must be technically deep and 100% accurate
- Shorter time slots than typical industry conferences (35 minutes including Q&A)

## Submitting a Winning Talk Proposal

**Resources for Writing Proposals**
- "How to Write with Style" by Vonnegut
- Talk Framework by goinggodotnet
- "Lachie's 7 Step Guide to Writing a Winning Tech Conference CFP"

**Strategy for Proposal Development**
- Seek feedback from experienced speakers and community leaders
- Be open to major revisions and direct feedback
- Work within character limits while making your proposal stand out
- Reach out to experienced speakers who volunteer their time

## Key Insight

Preparation is crucial -- the author went through "several sleepless nights" preparing for their first KubeCon talk despite extensive prior speaking experience. The difference with KubeCon is the combination of technical rigor required, expert audience, permanent recording, and time constraints.

## Session Formats at KubeCon
- Breakout sessions: 35 minutes including Q&A
- Deep dives: more technical, aimed at experienced users
- Tutorials: hands-on, longer format
- Lightning talks: 5 minutes
