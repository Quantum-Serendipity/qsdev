# Credential-Related Statistics from Verizon's 2025 DBIR

## Primary Breach Vectors
- **88% of attacks** against basic web applications involved stolen credentials
- **60% of all breaches** involved the human element (phishing, social engineering)
- Credential compromise remains "the number one initial access vector for breaches"

## Attack Pattern Evolution
- Brute force attacks against basic web apps **nearly tripled** in the past year (rising from ~20% to 60%)
- **2.8 billion passwords** were posted on criminal forums and darknet markets in 2024

## Compromised Data Composition
Beyond passwords alone, breached databases contained:
- Email addresses (61%)
- Phone numbers (39%)
- Government-issued IDs (22%)
- IP addresses (13%)
- Passports (1.8%)

## Password Quality Concerns
Only **3% of compromised passwords** met basic complexity requirements, indicating systemic weakness in credential management.

## MFA Bypass Techniques
When attackers encountered MFA on Microsoft 365 accounts:
- **31%** employed token theft
- **22%** used MFA interrupts/prompt bombing
- **9%** executed adversary-in-the-middle attacks

## Infostealer Malware Impact
Among darknet credential markets, infostealer logs contained:
- Social media logins (62%)
- Gaming accounts (49%)
- Streaming services (44%)
- Banking credentials (17%)

**Note:** The report does not provide time-to-detect statistics or comprehensive industry-specific breakdowns.
