## Core Research Citations

### Gloria Mark (UC Irvine)
- Knowledge workers experience interruptions every **6-12 minutes**
- Task switches occur every **3 minutes** on average
- Significant interruptions happen every **11 minutes**
- **23 minutes and 15 seconds** average to regain focus after interruption
- Source: Mark, G., et al. (2016), "The Cost of Interrupted Work"

### Sophie Leroy (University of Washington)
- "Attention residue" concept: performance remains impaired after task switches
- Greater impact from engaging, interrupted tasks
- Source: "Why Is It So Hard to Do My Work? The Challenge of Attention Residue when Switching Between Work Tasks"

### Parnin & DeLine (2010)
- Developers facing frequent interruptions show mental fatigue earlier in the day
- Results in more afternoon errors

### Amoroso d'Aragona et al. (2023)
- Frequent breaks correlate with more bugs due to lost cognitive context
- Prolonged activity gaps increase technical debt
- Interrupted coding sessions reduce maintainability, extending review cycles

### Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi (1990)
- Achieving flow requires ~15 minutes of uninterrupted work
- A single notification can instantly break flow state

## Cost Models

### 6-person team at €95/hour
- 7 interruptions/day x 12-min refocus = ~7 hours focus lost/dev/week (~€3,990/week)

### 10-person team at €110/hour
- 9 interruptions/day x 15-min refocus = ~11.25 hours focus lost/dev/week (~€12,375/week)

### ROI threshold
- 15% reduction in context switching for 10-person team recovers ~17 hours deep work weekly (~€7,400/month)

## Impact on Code Quality

One team implementing protected focus time saw:
- Story completion rate: **increased 35%**
- Bug reports: **decreased 28%**
- Team satisfaction scores: **improved 45%**

## Key Takeaway

Interrupted tasks take **twice as long** to finish and contain **twice as many errors**. Multitasking/switching can drain up to **40% of productivity** per day (Psychology Today).
