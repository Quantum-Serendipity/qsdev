# Product Demo Presentation Tips

## Core Preparation Strategy

The foundation of effective demos begins with thorough preparation. Luke Lowrey emphasizes a "demo preparation guide" covering research, planning, and practice techniques as the most valuable step for improvement.

## Team Approach

Rather than presenting solo, bringing a colleague offers significant advantages:
- Colleagues can monitor audience reactions and handle questions
- Two presenters allow one to control software while the other manages introductions
- Each team member should contribute meaningfully to avoid awkward silences
- Ideally, include both business and technical expertise

## Audience Engagement

**Opening strategy:** Keep ice-breaker moments brief and friendly without sacrificing demo time.

**Transparency:** "Let the audience know up front what you are going to show them." This prevents questions that jump ahead and allows viewers to highlight priorities.

**Language approach:** Use business terminology rather than technical jargon, framing features as user benefits ("As a [user type] I can...").

**Question management:** Balance answering inquiries with maintaining your planned narrative. Stop at natural workflow endpoints to invite discussion.

## Pacing and Delivery

A common mistake among developers involves moving too quickly. Strategic solutions include:
- Focusing on strongest features rather than demonstrating everything
- Marking key discussion points in your script
- Removing hands from keyboard/mouse during pauses to signal engagement
- Slowing deliberate mouse movements and avoiding distracting cursor flicking

## Remote Presentation Specifics

- Minimize shared screen content to essential windows
- Keep demo scripts visible beside your application
- Optimize window sizing and zoom levels beforehand
- State descriptive opening lines like "We are looking at the homepage..." rather than asking if viewers can see

## In-Person Delivery

- Position yourself at the room's front, facing the audience
- Use pauses as opportunities for eye contact and encouraging questions
- Bring backup cables, adapters, and internet connectivity options
