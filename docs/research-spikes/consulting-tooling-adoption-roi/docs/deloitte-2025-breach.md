# Deloitte 2025 Breach: Key Details

## What Happened
A threat actor using the alias "303" claimed responsibility for breaching Deloitte's internal systems on May 30, 2025, with the disclosure made on a dark web forum.

## Data Compromised
Two primary assets were accessed:
- **GitHub Credentials**: Found in public or poorly protected repositories, potentially enabling unauthorized access to development infrastructure, CI/CD pipelines, and private repositories
- **Proprietary Source Code**: Exfiltrated from internal project repositories belonging to Deloitte's U.S. consulting division

## How It Was Discovered
The breach was publicly disclosed when the threat actor posted claims on a dark web forum. Early security analyses later supported these allegations.

## Timeline & Scope
- **Date**: May 30, 2025
- **Scope**: Deloitte's U.S. consulting division specifically
- **Nature**: GitHub credentials indicate that attackers may have exploited weak authentication mechanisms or insufficiently secured repositories

## Impact
Potential consequences include:
- Unauthorized access to internal development environments
- Identification of software vulnerabilities
- Reputational damage to a trusted consulting leader
- Regulatory scrutiny under GDPR and other data protection laws

## Connection to Credential Management
The breach reflects historical credential management failures. In 2017, Deloitte's internal VPN passwords and operational information were found in a public GitHub repository, indicating systemic challenges in securing credentials.
