## The 3x Rule (Most Common)

The average billing rate is 3x the salary of the person doing the work. The 3x multiplier factors in:
- 1/3 covers gross pay
- 1/3 covers benefits, overhead, and employer obligations
- 1/3 covers profit margin

## Variations

**3x-4x Range:**
- 3x is baseline for most professional services
- 4x applies when utilization is lower or overhead is higher
- At 85% billable utilization, the 3-4x range works well
- Law firms: new associates need to generate 5x their salary

**Overhead Multiplier:**
- Typical overhead: 50-100% of gross wages
- Covers office space, equipment, tools, employer costs
- Overhead Rate = Total Operating Expenses (excluding Direct Labor) / Direct Labor

**Deltek Benchmark (from earlier search):**
- Fringe: 35%
- Overhead: 25%
- G&A: 18%
- Cumulative cost multiplier: 1.99x (each employee costs roughly 2x base salary)
- Billing rate then adds margin on top of this loaded cost

## Fully Loaded Cost Calculation

Components:
1. Base salary/wages
2. Employee benefits (health insurance, retirement)
3. Payroll taxes (FICA, FUTA, state unemployment)
4. Overhead expenses (rent, utilities, admin support)
5. Indirect costs (training, recruitment, support services)

Formula: Sum all costs / productive hours = hourly fully loaded cost rate

## Utilization Reality Check

Consultants average ~70% utilization, which means the multiplier must account for 30% non-billable time. A consultant earning $150K/year with 70% utilization has an effective cost basis of $214K/year against billable hours.
