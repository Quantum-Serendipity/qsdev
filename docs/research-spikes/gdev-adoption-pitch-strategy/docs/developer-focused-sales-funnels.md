# Developer-Focused Sales Funnels: A Hybrid Approach

## Core Concept

Developer tool companies face a unique challenge: merging bottom-up organic adoption with traditional top-down sales approaches. The proposal is viewing these dual motions through a two-dimensional matrix rather than a linear funnel.

## The Two Sales Motions

**Bottom-Up Developer Motion (DEBS):**
Discover (organic/community awareness) → Evaluate (documentation/free trials) → Build (prototypes/POCs) → Scale (monetization/upselling)

**Top-Down Sales Motion:**
Prospect → Lead → Opportunity → Customer, driven by SDRs and Account Executives targeting decision-makers with budgets.

## Key Differences from Traditional B2B

Unlike standard SaaS models, "a qualified account in a developer software company will have a lot of developer activity" that requires measurement. Developer software purchases inherently require developer validation — teams won't adopt tools their engineers haven't vetted.

## The Two-Dimensional Matrix Framework

Plot accounts on axes measuring:
- **X-axis:** Sales team conversational progress
- **Y-axis:** Developer adoption and activity levels

This reveals strategic insights across quadrants, enabling targeted interventions by different teams.

## Team-Specific Strategies

**Marketing:** Target tech decision-makers in high-developer-activity accounts
**Developer Relations:** Identify power users as advocates; activate low-adoption accounts
**Sales/Customer Success:** Identify expansion-ready and conversion-ready accounts
**RevOps:** Detect pipeline imbalances between top-down and bottom-up motions
