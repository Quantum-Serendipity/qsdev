# Attention Curve in Presentations
- **Source**: https://www.presentationload.com/blog/attention-curve-presentations/
- **Retrieved**: 2026-05-15

## Key Finding on Attention Decline
The audience's attention during a PowerPoint presentation is high at the beginning but decreases after the first 10-15 minutes.

## Strategies to Support the Attention Curve

**Repetition Technique:**
Use phrases like "Let me summarize what I just said," "The main points are," and "To summarize" to reinforce key messages through repetition, as people retain information better when it's repeated.

**Segmentation Approach:**
Rather than presenting consecutively, divide your presentations into smaller sections with a short break between each to maintain engagement throughout.

**Ending Strategy:**
Your ending is just as important as your introduction and should avoid introducing new information — instead, restate key points in different words.

## Three Core Techniques to Leave Lasting Impact

1. **Summarize findings** concisely into one or two statements
2. **Include a call-to-action** directing audience response
3. **Share personal stories** that emotionally connect to your message

## Additional Attention-Supporting Methods

- Organize material clearly with signposts
- Appeal to multiple senses through images and sounds
- Encourage questions and active participation
- Follow up with resources or summary handouts

The overarching message: structure presentations around the documented attention decline pattern to maximize retention.
