# Y Combinator's Complete Guide to Demo Day Presentations

- **Source**: https://www.ycombinator.com/blog/guide-to-demo-day-pitches/
- **Retrieved**: 2026-05-15

---

## Core Purpose
Demo day presentations aim to intrigue investors enough to secure follow-up meetings rather than immediate investment decisions. The presentation serves as your company's foundational story.

## Four Core Qualities of Effective Presentations
1. **Clarity** – presented simply and understandably
2. **Excitement** – compelling opportunity demanding attention
3. **Informative** – teaches audience something new
4. **Memorable** – audience recalls your company and mission later

## The "Vertebrae" Framework
Identify 3-4 key points audiences will remember using these questions:
- What are we building and for whom?
- Why hasn't this been done before?
- Why is our approach difficult?
- Why is this an unmissable opportunity?

Begin narrowly, expand only as necessary.

## Presentation Structure Components

**Introduction**
Clearly state what you do in understandable terms. "We deliver groceries to homes" beats "AI-based grocery need resolvers."

**Problem**
Describe the problem you're solving early—don't bury this until midway through.

**Product & Customer**
Explain what you're building and target users in detail. Modern demos rarely work; clear verbal explanation suffices.

**Opportunity (TAM)**
Use concrete, bottom-up calculations: "Price point $X × Y potential customers = addressable market." Avoid nebulous top-down projections.

**Traction**
Show growth metrics and hockey-stick curves. Pre-traction companies must rely more heavily on founder credentials and compelling storytelling.

**Team**
Highlight only genuinely notable credentials ("holds patent on core technology").

**Business Model**
Ensure audiences understand profitability and unit economics by presentation's end.

**Conclusion**
End powerfully, explicitly restating your 3-4 key vertebrae.

## Slide Guidelines

**Design Principles:**
- One main point per slide maximum
- Prefer relevant images over words
- Keep text to 7 words or fewer, large font
- Ensure readability from auditorium's back row
- Place important text at slide top
- Use high-contrast colors (primary colors/black preferred)
- Avoid cumulative graphs (always appear suspiciously growth-oriented)

**Critical Rule:** Audiences cannot simultaneously read slides and listen. Simple, glanceable slides are essential—complex ones prompt email checking.

## Preparation Strategy

**Practice Intensively**
Practice repeatedly with co-founders and others willing to give honest feedback. Recording yourself reveals delivery issues you won't otherwise notice.

**Presenter Selection**
Choose your clearest, most confident speaker—not necessarily the CEO. Presentation quality matters more than ego.

## Speaking Technique

**Delivery Qualities:**
- Project confidence and passion
- Speak unnaturally slowly (audiences absorb vast information)
- Pause deliberately for emphasis and processing time
- Speak clearly, especially if English isn't your native language
- Maintain natural cadence despite memorization
- Use pauses strategically

**Movement Guidelines:**
- Stand with feet planted, weight distributed equally
- Avoid swaying, pacing, or repetitive gestures
- Face your audience consistently
- Move deliberately, stopping between movements
- Minimal arm gestures; avoid dancing movements

## Common Fatal Errors

- Unclear problem description or solution explanation
- Unnecessary jargon, marketing boilerplate, or complex phrasing
- Exaggeration or dishonesty
- Failing to clearly convey why investors should care
- Conflicting audience attention between slides and presenter
- Looking at slides while speaking
- Speaking too fast or using excessive words
- Including irrelevant slides
- Using videos (investors want to judge you live)
- Unpaused delivery without breathing room
- Displaying boredom, anger, or lack of passion

## Key Insight from Geoff Ralston

"Practice" is the singular most important preparation technique. Working thoroughly on your story improves both delivery and persuasiveness through iterative refinement.
