## Key Timeline Metrics

**Traditional vs. Fast Onboarding:**
- Traditional onboarding: 30-90 days
- Structured staff augmentation: 7-14 days
- Case study achievement: 14 days to 110% productivity

## Case Study: 4 Developers Over 14 Days

**Background:**
A Series B SaaS company with 25 engineers needed to double capacity within 10 weeks for a product launch. They faced a 70-day deadline versus the typical 90-day hiring cycle.

**Week-by-Week Results:**

| Metric | Week 1 | Week 2 | Week 3 |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| Commits | 12 | 28 | 34 |
| PRs Merged | 2 | 7 | 9 |
| Productivity Level | 70% | 100%+ | 110% |
| Features Shipped | 0 | 1 | 2 |

**Critical Timeline:**
- Day 1: Kickoff and environment setup (0 commits)
- Day 2: First commit (CSS bug fix)
- Day 3: First PR merged to production staging
- Week 2: Architectural contributions and feature development
- Week 3: Two production features shipped (user role management, real-time notifications)

## 4-Hour Preparation Framework

Essential pre-launch activities:
- Architecture overview call (90 minutes)
- Documentation sharing and access provisioning
- Week 1 task definition (3 bugs + 1 small feature)
- Tool access completion 3 days early

## Success Factors

**Required conditions (80% success rate):**
- Senior developers (7+ years experience)
- Clear initial tasks documented
- 4+ hours daily time zone overlap
- Supportive existing team
- Basic architecture documentation

**Failure indicators (20% of cases):**
Missing documentation, junior developers, hostile team environments, or async-only communication extend timelines to 3-4 weeks.

## ROI Results (6 months)

- $180K cost savings
- $500K revenue captured from on-time launch
- 0% turnover among initial 4 developers
- Team velocity increased from 23 to 45 story points
