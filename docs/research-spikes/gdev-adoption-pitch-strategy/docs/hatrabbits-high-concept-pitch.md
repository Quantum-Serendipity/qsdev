## Core Concept

The High Concept Pitch is a "super-condensed elevator pitch" consisting of three words using the "X for Y analogy" format. It originated in Hollywood as a method for producers to pitch projects by positioning them against something widely recognized.

## Famous Examples

**Film Industry:**
- *Alien* was pitched as "Jaws from space"
- *Hotel Rwanda* was promoted as "Schindler's List in Rwanda"

**Tech/Business:**
- Brenger: "The Uber for big stuff"
- Scribd: "Netflix for Books"
- Sniffer: "Tinder for dogs & dog lovers"

## Five Key Benefits

1. **Easy to explain** – Three words plus one clarifying sentence convey your concept simply
2. **Easy to understand** – Leverages familiar references so audiences grasp what you do immediately
3. **Proof of potential** – Positioning against successful examples demonstrates viability
4. **Easy to share** – The condensed format encourages others to spread your message organically
5. **Creates curiosity** – Compels audiences to learn more about your full concept

## Crafting Guidelines

- Keep it short using the X for Y formula
- Reference something successful and widely known
- Follow with a killer elevator pitch
- Use only as explanation, not as your slogan
