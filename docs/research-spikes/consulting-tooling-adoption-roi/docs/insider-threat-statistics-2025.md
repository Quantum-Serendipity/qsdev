# Insider Threat Statistics 2025

## Annual Costs
- **Global average:** $17.4 million per organization annually (2025)
- **North America:** $22.2 million average
- **Growth trend:** 109% increase since 2018

## Cost by Incident Type
- **Credential theft (compromised insiders):** $779,797 per incident (costliest)
- **Malicious insider:** $715,366 per incident; IBM reports $4.92 million per breach
- **Negligent insider:** $676,517 per incident; $8.8 million total annual cost per org

## Incident Frequency
- **Organizations affected:** 83% experienced at least one insider attack in the past year
- **Negligent incidents:** Average of 13.5 per organization annually
- **Malicious incidents:** Average of 6.3 per organization annually
- **Compromised insider incidents:** Average of 4.8 per organization annually
- **Volume trend:** Organizations experiencing 11-20 attacks annually saw fivefold increase between 2023-2024

## Detection & Containment Timeline
- **Average containment time:** 81 days
- **Cost correlation:** Incidents contained under 31 days average $10.6 million; over 91 days reach $18.7 million
- **Reactive spending gap:** Organizations spend $211,021 on containment but only $37,756 on proactive monitoring

## Common Causes (Accidental/Negligent)
Human error is the primary driver with 45% of employees citing distraction as their reason for falling for phishing scams. Accidental exposure includes misconfigured security settings, sending sensitive data to wrong recipients, and phishing link clicks.

Human error was involved in an estimated 43% of data breaches generally.
