# Service Provider Liability in Managed Services Agreements

## Typical Liability Structure

Standard managed services agreements employ a two-tiered liability model. Third-party indemnification obligations generally remain uncapped for specific breaches like intellectual property infringement or confidentiality violations. However, each party's liability to the other for direct damages arising out of a breach of the agreement is capped, generally to a multiple of charges incurred.

## Data Security as a Special Category

The framework distinguishes data security breaches as exceptionally serious. Parties may claim indirect or consequential damages specifically for data security and privacy incidents, subject to a separately negotiated cap that typically exceeds the standard damages limitation.

## MSSP-Specific Complications

Managed security service provider agreements expose critical gaps in traditional liability structures. A service provider's failure to properly implement security measures constitutes a service obligation breach rather than a data security breach — meaning clients face the lower damages cap despite potentially severe downstream consequences.

The article identifies a problematic scenario: If the MSSP is required to inform its client about a security failure but fails to do so, it has again breached its service obligations. This notification failure can trigger client liability to third parties while the MSSP's damages remain capped at lower levels.

## Key Negotiation Points

Clients should resist broad indemnities favoring service providers and seek exemptions for provider breaches of care standards. The article warns against accepting "pass-through" liability terms from original equipment manufacturers and emphasizes that clients expect providers to stand behind their solutions.
