# Sales One-Pager Template: The Leave-Behind That Closes Deals
- **Source**: https://discover.gtmplaybook.co/sales-one-pager-template
- **Retrieved**: 2026-05-15

## Core Purpose
A one-pager functions as an internal selling document — "the document a buyer forwards to their boss to get budget approval." It's not a brochure but a decision-support tool that helps champions justify purchases internally.

## Six-Section Anatomy

### Section 1: The Problem
Lead with pain rather than product features. Structure as:
- Quantified outcome statement (time, money, or risk impact)
- Why existing solutions fail
- Cost of inaction

Example approach: "Compliance reviews delay product launches by 4-6 weeks. Manual workflows create bottlenecks slowing revenue. Without systematic processes, every launch becomes a negotiation."

### Section 2: The Solution
Deliver in one sentence maximum using this template: "[Product] helps [ICP] to [outcome] through [approach]."

Example: "ComplianceOS helps B2B SaaS teams ship compliant products in days through automated legal review workflows."

### Section 3: How It Works
Present exactly three steps — no more, no fewer. Five steps create confusion; one feels insufficient.

### Section 4: Outcomes
Quantify improvements using customer data or industry benchmarks:
- Launch timelines reduce from 6 weeks to 5 days
- Legal teams save 20 hours per release
- Compliance risk drops 80% through automation

### Section 5: Proof
Deploy social proof via:
- 3-6 recognizable customer logos from buyer's industry
- Case study snippets showing specific results
- Third-party validation (awards, ratings)

### Section 6: Next Step
Provide specific, low-friction CTAs. Replace "Contact us to learn more" with "Schedule a 20-minute demo to see how we handle your compliance requirements."

## Design Principles

**Skimmability:** Buyers scan 30-60 seconds before deciding depth. Use large bold headlines, bullet points, and prominent statistics.

**White Space:** Target 60% text coverage and 40% white space. Dense layouts "look desperate."

**Branding:** Logo appears once in corner; use brand colors for accents only, not throughout.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Leading with features instead of outcomes
- Using generic stock photography
- Unclear or missing CTAs
- Attempting comprehensive product explanation

## Stage-Specific Variations

**Discovery:** Problem-first emphasis with minimal product details
**Evaluation:** Differentiation focus with proof points and case studies
**Procurement:** ROI-first framing with TCO, payback period, and contract terms

## Distribution Strategy
Deploy at three moments:
1. Post-discovery as follow-up
2. 24 hours before demos
3. During procurement phase for champion forwarding

Track forward rates — if buyers aren't sharing internally, the one-pager underperforms.

## Governance Model
- Assign single accountable owner
- Monthly hygiene checks (links, examples, claims)
- Quarterly strategic reviews
- Event-driven updates (launches, pricing changes, competitive shifts)
