# 3-Act Structure for Business Presentations (Duarte)
- **Source**: https://www.duarte.com/blog/business-communication-demands-3-act-story-structure/
- **Retrieved**: 2026-05-15

## Framework Overview

The 3-act structure divides narratives into three distinct phases: setup, confrontation, and resolution. According to Duarte's methodology, this framework transforms "disparate data and facts into a compelling narrative that engages, persuades, and inspires audiences."

## The Three Acts

**Act 1: Setup**
- Introduce characters, setting, and central conflict
- Position the audience as the hero, not yourself
- Highlight their challenges and struggles
- Establish why the presentation matters to them

**Act 2: Confrontation**
- Develop complexity through additional considerations and conflicts
- Incorporate relevant data alongside mini-stories and anecdotes
- Use contrast between "what is" and "what could be"
- Address roadblocks and obstacles
- Balance analytical content with emotional examples

**Act 3: Resolution**
- Present your solution and demonstrate effectiveness
- Illustrate how it addresses the problem and improves lives
- Include a clear call to action (download, schedule meeting, invest, etc.)
- Provide closure and memorable takeaways

## Key Principles

**Contrast as Engagement Tool**
"Presentations without contrast are boring." Effective Act 2 development oscillates between challenges and solutions, problems and opportunities, obstacles and rewards.

**Avoid Common Mistakes**
- Don't position yourself or your company as the hero
- Don't present only data without narrative structure
- Don't use typical sales pitch formulas ("benefit, benefit, we're the best")

## Emotional and Logical Integration

Success requires balancing "analytical/quantitative content with emotional/qualitative content." This dual approach helps connect with audiences on multiple levels while maintaining credibility and relatability.

## Business Applications

**Internal Presentations**: Context -> analysis with solutions -> final proposal with action items
**Investor Pitches**: Compelling problem/opportunity -> detailed product case -> ROI summary with persuasive ask
**Keynote Speeches**: Powerful opening anecdote -> theme development through stories and data -> visionary conclusion with call to action

## Measurable Impact

The framework leverages storytelling's documented effectiveness: "stories are remembered 22 times more than facts." This structural approach supports retention and audience motivation to act.

## Alignment with Duarte Method

The 3-act structure integrates with Duarte's methodology through:
- Audience-first empathy and understanding
- Big Idea Formula (point of view plus stakes)
- Presentation Sparkline Framework (oscillating contrast)
- Rhetorical Triangle (logos, pathos, ethos balance)
