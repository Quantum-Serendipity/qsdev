## Ramp-Up Time Benchmarks
- **Average duration**: 3-9 months to reach full productivity
- **Extended cases**: Significant percentage of companies requiring a full year
- **Initial productivity**: ~1 month before new hires contribute meaningful value

## Productivity Impact Calculation
The article presents a concrete example: A rapidly growing late-stage startup hiring 100 engineers annually could gain **~17 additional developer-years** by cutting full ramp-up time in half (from 6 months to 3 months), representing millions in salary savings alone.

## Retention Context
Engineers staying approximately 2 years means those requiring 12-month ramp-up periods spend "half their time being merely partially productive" before departure.

## Measurement Methods
The author recommends three approaches:
1. **Survey team leads** on average independence timelines
2. **Map key proficiencies** by listing required competencies and measuring time-to-mastery
3. **Guesstimate** using managerial intuition about realistic ranges

## Research Basis
Data derived from surveying 80+ engineers and engineering managers across varying company sizes about their onboarding experiences.
