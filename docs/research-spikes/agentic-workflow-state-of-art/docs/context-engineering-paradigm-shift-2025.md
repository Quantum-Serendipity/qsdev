## The Paradigm Shift
Context engineering emerged in mid-2025 as the evolutionary successor to prompt engineering. The term became settled in June 2025 when Shopify CEO Tobi Lutke and former OpenAI researcher Andrej Karpathy publicly endorsed it.

## Key Distinction
- **Prompt Engineering**: Focuses on one-time textual instructions given to an LLM
- **Context Engineering**: Focuses on the contextual information architecture for ongoing interactions

Prompt Engineering is a **subset** of Context Engineering, not the other way around. It's one small part of the much bigger machine Context Engineering builds.

## Why the Shift Occurred
In early experimentation, prompt engineering sufficed. As organizations move from pilots to production, prompt engineering alone cannot deliver the accuracy, memory, or governance required in complex environments.

## Key Statistics
- LangChain's 2025 State of Agent Engineering: 57% of organizations now have AI agents in production
- 32% cite quality as top barrier
- Most failures traced to poor context management, not LLM capabilities

## Context Engineering Components
Beyond the prompt itself, context engineering manages:
- Retrieved documents and tool outputs
- Conversation history and memory
- State management across sessions
- Structured metadata and schemas
- Trust hierarchies for different information sources

## Enterprise Trajectory
Gartner predicts context engineering will move from innovation differentiator to foundational element of enterprise AI infrastructure in 12-18 months (from mid-2025).
