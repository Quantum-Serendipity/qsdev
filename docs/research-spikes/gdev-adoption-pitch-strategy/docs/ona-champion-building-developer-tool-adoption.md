# Champion Building for Developer Tool Adoption

## Core Strategy Overview

Champion building is a socio-technical approach to successful developer tool adoption. Rather than relying solely on broad visibility and excitement, this methodology emphasizes building deep support within your organization through targeted champions.

## Three-Step Framework

### Step 1: Identify Internal Champions
Select motivated, influential engineers who are "dreamers" believing in future possibilities. These can be tech leads, principal engineers, or junior engineers eager to prove themselves -- individuals who don't settle for the status quo.

### Step 2: Build Compelling Use Cases
Work closely with champions on 1-2 projects that:
- Solve obvious business pain points
- Fall within the champion's sphere of influence
- Benefit from their day-to-day involvement

The article emphasizes: "Pull them close. Not just metaphorically, but physically. If you're a co-located company, sit physically with the champion(s)."

**Key outcomes to achieve:**
- Develop institutional knowledge through frequent, practical tool usage
- Create organization-specific documentation connecting the tool to company context
- Document frequently asked questions and provide copy-paste examples

### Step 3: Expand and Scale
Once you have documented use cases and knowledgeable champions, leverage them for visibility. Have champions present success stories rather than platform teams doing demonstrations.

## Critical Warnings

**Avoid the "All-Hands Demo Trap"**: Running early, broad demos fails because excitement fades quickly when competing with urgent business priorities. "Business priorities always win."

Instead of assuming visibility drives adoption, recognize this as fundamentally a people and technology challenge requiring intentional engagement.

## Success Metrics

- Champions answering peers' questions without documentation
- Social proof from initial users influencing broader adoption
- Documented best practices reducing support burden
- Organic adoption spreading team-to-team
