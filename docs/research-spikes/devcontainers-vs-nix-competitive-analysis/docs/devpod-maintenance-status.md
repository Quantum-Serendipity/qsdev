# DevPod — Maintenance Status and Sustainability Concerns
- **Sources**:
  - https://github.com/loft-sh/devpod/issues/1915 ("Still Maintained?" — opened Sept 11, 2025)
  - https://github.com/loft-sh/devpod/issues/1946 ("Community Devpod" — opened Nov 30, 2025)
  - Web search results from multiple sources
- **Retrieved**: 2026-03-20

## Timeline

- **May 2023**: DevPod launched by Loft Labs as open-source project
- **2023-2025**: Active development with 210 releases through v0.6.15 (March 2025)
- **April 2025**: Last significant activity / release (v0.6.15)
- **August 2025**: Loft Labs rebrands to "vCluster Labs" — vCluster becomes their primary focus
- **September 2025**: Issue #1915 opened asking "Still Maintained?" — no changes or releases in months
- **November 2025**: Issue #1946 "Community Devpod" opened — community member forks project and ships upgrades

## Key Findings

### From Issue #1915 ("Still Maintained?")
- Opened by brunocascio on September 11, 2025
- Noted no changes or releases in past months
- Asked whether project is still maintained or planned for deprecation
- Appears to still be open with no assignments or labels

### From Issue #1946 ("Community Devpod")
- The DevPod team's attention is focused on vcluster for the foreseeable future
- Lack of engagement from original developers in both repository and Slack channel
- Team stated they "don't have the bandwidth to properly review and verify changes" and therefore cannot merge PRs
- A community member forked the project and shipped upgrades
- Plans to review/merge PRs from the backlog
- Looking into setting up SSL and Apple Developer accounts for software signing if enough interest exists

### Company Context
- Loft Labs rebranded to vCluster Labs in August 2025
- vCluster is clearly the company's priority product
- DevPod was likely a complementary project that didn't generate sufficient commercial traction
- DevPod Pro / DevPod Engine (enterprise offerings) appear to have been deprioritized

## Assessment

DevPod is functionally stable at v0.6.15 but is **effectively unmaintained** by the original team as of mid-2025. A community fork exists but its sustainability is uncertain. The MPL-2.0 license permits forking and continued development. For any tool evaluation, the lack of active maintenance is a significant risk factor.
