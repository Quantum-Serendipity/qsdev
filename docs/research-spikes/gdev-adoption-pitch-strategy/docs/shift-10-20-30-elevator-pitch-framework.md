# The 10-to-20-to-30 Elevator Pitch Framework

- **Source**: https://www.shiftcomm.com/thinking/10-to-20-to-30-an-elevator-pitch-framework
- **Retrieved**: 2026-05-15

---

## Core Concept

SHIFT Communications presents a pitch structure based on attention span decline. Modern audiences have shrunk from 12-second to 8-second attention spans, requiring a tighter approach than traditional "thirty-second pitches."

## The Framework Structure

The framework operates in three escalating stages:

**Stage 1 (10 seconds/20 words):** This opening is critical. The article emphasizes that "most people decide whether you're worth their actual attention in ten seconds." Craft your initial 20 words to capture genuine interest, not mere politeness.

**Stage 2 (20 seconds/40 words):** If you succeed with the first segment, you earn another 20-word opportunity to deepen engagement.

**Stage 3 (30 seconds/60 words):** Success at 40 words grants access to the full 30-second window.

## Key Principles

The article stresses that "people might give you thirty seconds of their time, but they may not give you thirty seconds of their attention." Recipients will listen politely but may not truly engage unless you grip them immediately.

## Practical Application

Write three separate pitches in 20-word increments. While condensing your message is uncomfortable, the alternative—losing your audience's genuine attention—carries greater consequences.

The framework prioritizes concision, storytelling authenticity, and avoiding industry jargon across all three tiers.
