# Deloitte Breach Details

## What Happened
Deloitte's internal email systems and all administrator accounts were compromised. A source with direct knowledge stated: "They accessed the entire email database and all admin accounts."

## Discovery
The breach likely began in fall 2016, with Deloitte issuing a mandatory password reset to all U.S. employees on October 13, 2016—suggesting this was when the intrusion was first detected.

## Data Compromised
- Entire internal email system
- All administrator account credentials
- Client information across multiple sectors
- Personal data on blue-chip clients
- Material from company email systems of government departments and major corporations

## Scope
The breach was far more extensive than Deloitte's public statements suggested. The insider noted the company accessed "the entire email database," contradicting Deloitte's claim that "very few" clients were impacted.

## Affected Accounts
The source confirmed compromise of all administrator accounts company-wide, plus the entire email infrastructure serving thousands of employees globally.

## Timeline
- **Fall 2016**: Intrusion believed to have begun
- **October 13, 2016**: Mandatory password reset issued
- **September 2017**: Breach publicly disclosed

## Response
Deloitte mobilized cybersecurity experts and contacted authorities. The investigation focused on the Nashville office. However, investigators remained uncertain whether intruders were completely removed from systems.

## Client Impact
Six clients were officially notified, though Deloitte's Cyber Intelligence Centre serves major corporations like FedEx and Invesco—creating potential exposure concerns.
