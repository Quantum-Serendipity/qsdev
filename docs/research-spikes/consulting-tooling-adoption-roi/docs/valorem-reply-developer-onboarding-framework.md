## Key Time Metrics

**Standard vs. Optimized Onboarding:**
- Poor process: 6-12 weeks to productivity
- Structured framework: 2-3 weeks to meaningful productivity, 30-45 days to full independence
- Environment setup alone: 2-5 days (manual) vs. 15 minutes (automated)

## Specific Benchmarks

**First meaningful code contribution:**
- Fast teams: days 3-5
- Slow teams: week 2-3

**Productivity ramp:**
- 4-6 weeks for new developers to become productive (industry standard)
- Senior engineers spend 15-20 hours answering basic questions

**Daily question metric:**
- Should decrease from 20+ questions day one to 2-3 by day 15

## Retention & Cost Impact

- Organizations with poor onboarding lose 25% of technical hires within first year
- 86% of new hires decide whether they'll stay long-term within six months
- Replacement cost: $200-300K per lost developer
- Structured onboarding improves retention from 60-70% to 85%+
- Represents a 40% reduction in first-year attrition

## 30-60-90 Day Framework (Valorem's model)

Days 1-30 focus on foundation and first pull request; days 31-60 emphasize active contribution; days 61-90 target independent ownership matching experienced developer velocity.
