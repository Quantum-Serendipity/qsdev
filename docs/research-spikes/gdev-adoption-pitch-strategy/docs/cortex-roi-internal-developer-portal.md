# ROI Assessment Framework for Internal Developer Portals

## Key Impact Areas

### 1. Developer Productivity Enhancement

**Measurement Approach:**
The framework uses productive development hours to quantify impact. Industry research shows developers spend 10-25 hours weekly on actual coding, with the remainder consumed by meetings, administrative tasks, and context switching.

**Formula:** Current productive hours + improvement factor = future state

**Impact Factors:**
- Conservative approach: 5% improvement
- Industry benchmark (Forrester): "20% improvement in developer productivity"
- Organization-specific adjustments based on unique use cases

**Financial Calculation:** Multiply productivity hours gained annually by average developer salary to determine cost savings.

### 2. Accelerated Developer Onboarding

**Benchmark Data:**
New developers typically require "3 to 6 months" to reach full productivity. An IDP can reduce time-to-first-commit to approximately one week.

**Key Variables:**
- Number of planned hires annually
- Current onboarding timeline
- Target onboarding timeline with IDP
- Average developer compensation

**Value Calculation:** (Current weeks to productivity - Target weeks) x number of new hires x weekly developer cost

### 3. Faster Migration & Modernization Initiatives

**Acceleration Metrics:**
- Forrester data: "25%" reduction in deployment time
- Real-world case study: One customer reduced Kubernetes migration timeline from 24 to 16 months

### 4. Automated Production Readiness Reviews

**Manual Process Costs:**
Calculate hours spent on:
- Spreadsheet-based checklists
- Status update meetings
- Compliance verification
- Recurring health checks/audits

**Savings Formula:** (Hours per review x number of annual reviews x reviewer hourly rate)

## Presentation Strategy

**Leadership Alignment:**
Focus metrics on organizational priorities. Quantify impact in the areas most relevant to your decision-makers -- whether cost reduction, speed-to-revenue, or risk mitigation.

**Conservative Approach:**
Start with modest improvement percentages against industry benchmarks, then adjust based on internal assessment of unique use cases and current-state gaps.
