# AWS Misconfiguration Cost Data and Statistics

## Data Breach Costs
- **Average US cost**: $9.44 million per breach
- **Global average**: $4.35 million per breach
- Source: IBM report cited in article

## High-Profile Breach Examples
- **Capital One (2019)**: Exposed PII of 100 million individuals
- **2020 European retailer**: Millions of customer records compromised
- **FlexBooker (2021)**: 3 million users affected
- **SeniorAdvisor (2021)**: Personal data of 3+ million people exposed
- **Pegasus Airlines (2022)**: 23 million files exposed

## Downtime Costs
Enterprise-level impacts:
- **Apple (2015)**: $25 million lost after 12 hours of Apple Store downtime
- **Delta Airlines (2016)**: $150 million lost after 5 hours of downtime (2,000 canceled flights)
- **Facebook (2019)**: $90 million lost after 14 hours of downtime

General estimates:
- Large enterprise: ~$9,000 per minute ($540,000/hour)
- Small business: ~$427 per minute ($25,620/hour)

## Regulatory Fine Ranges
- Brazil (LGPD): $9 million or more
- Canada (CPPA): $25 million or more
- EU (GDPR): $21 million or more
- South Korea (PIPA): $50 million or more

## Misconfiguration Frequency
Nearly 70% of exposed records -- 5.4 billion in total -- were caused by unintentional internet exposure due to misconfigurations.
