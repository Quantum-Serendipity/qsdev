# Consultancy Fees & Rates
- **Source**: https://www.consultancy.org/consulting-industry/fees-rates
- **Retrieved**: 2026-03-20

## Revenue Per Consultant Per Annum (By Firm Tier)

**Global Strategy Consulting Firms** (McKinsey, BCG, Bain, Strategy&, Roland Berger, A.T. Kearney)
- Positioned at the highest tier; specific figures not publicly disclosed

**Global Functional Specialists** (Hay Group, Mercer, Willis Towers Watson, FTI, Navigant)
- $300,000-$400,000 per consultant annually

**The Big Four** (Deloitte, PwC, EY, KPMG)
- Competitive in the $300,000-$400,000 range (varies by service line)
- Strategy/management consulting practices command premium rates
- Lower rates for accounting, audit, operational IT services

**Small/Mid-size Technology Players & Mid-size Global Accountants** (BDO, Baker Tilly, Grant Thornton, RSM, PKF)
- $150,000-$220,000 per consultant annually

**Global Technology Firms** (Atos, Accenture, Capgemini, CGI)
- $150,000-$220,000 baseline (consulting practices significantly higher)

**Independent Contractors**
- Below $50,000 to above $400,000 annually (no ceiling)

## Hourly Rate Range
- £50 for an interim consultant working on an operational level
- £300+ for a consultant from a leading strategy consulting firm

## Key Limitation
This represents "a simplified and indicative view" with acknowledged exceptions across regions and service offerings. Consulting firms regard their rates as one of their key competitive assets and manage their fee structure as a 'trade secret'.
