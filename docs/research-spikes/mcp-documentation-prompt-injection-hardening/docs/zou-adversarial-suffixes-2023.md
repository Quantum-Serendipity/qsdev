# Universal and Transferable Adversarial Attacks on Aligned Language Models

## Authors
Andy Zou, Zifan Wang, Nicholas Carlini, Milad Nasr, J. Zico Kolter, Matt Fredrikson

## Overview

The paper presents an automated attack method that generates adversarial suffixes to bypass safety measures in aligned language models. The approach combines "greedy and gradient-based search techniques" to create prompts that induce models to generate harmful content rather than refusing requests.

## Attack Methodology (Greedy Coordinate Gradient / GCG)

The method automatically produces adversarial suffixes that can be appended to queries requesting objectionable content. These suffixes are designed to maximize the probability of affirmative responses from the model. Unlike traditional jailbreaks, these are built in an entirely automated fashion, allowing one to create a virtually unlimited number of such attacks.

## Key Transferability Results

When the attack suffix was trained on multiple prompts and models (Vicuna-7B and 13B), it successfully induced harmful outputs in:

- **Proprietary models**: ChatGPT, Bard, and Claude
- **Open-source models**: LLaMA-2-Chat, Pythia, Falcon, and others

"Adversarial prompts generated by our approach are quite transferable, including to black-box, publicly released LLMs."

## Security Implications

The research demonstrates fundamental vulnerabilities in aligned language systems and raises critical questions about preventing aligned models from producing objectionable content when exposed to adversarial suffixes.
