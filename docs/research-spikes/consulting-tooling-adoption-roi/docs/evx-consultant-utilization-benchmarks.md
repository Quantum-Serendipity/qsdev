# Measuring Consultant Utilization: Definitions, Calculations & Best Practices
- **Source**: https://www.evxsoftware.com/blog/utilization-in-consulting-how-to-measure-improve-and-optimize-consultant-utilization-rates
- **Retrieved**: 2026-03-20

## Industry-Wide Benchmarks

**General Target Range:** Most consulting firms aim for utilization rates between 70% to 85%

**Firm-Wide Average:** The average utilization target across all employees tends to fall between 70% and 80%

**Technical/Engineering Firms:** Targets skew closer to the 80% mark

## Benchmarks by Seniority Level

| Role | Target Range | Key Focus |
|------|--------------|-----------|
| Junior Consultants | 65-75% | Higher non-billable time for training and mentoring |
| Mid-Level Consultants | 75-85% | Client-facing work and project execution |
| Senior Consultants/Managers | 80-90% | Project leadership and client consultations |
| Partners/Executives | 60-75% | Business development and strategic work (non-billable focus) |

## Core Utilization Formula

**Basic:** number of hours doing work / total number of hours available = UTILIZATION

**Billable utilization:** number of billable hours performed / total available hours

## Practical Example

For a full-time consultant (160 hours/month, 40 hours/week):
- Billable hours: 120
- Available hours: 140 (excluding 20 PTO)
- Result: (120/140) x 100 = 85.7% utilization

## Key Financial Insight

Firms measuring utilization tend to be in the group of the most profitable ones — direct correlation between tracking and profitability.
