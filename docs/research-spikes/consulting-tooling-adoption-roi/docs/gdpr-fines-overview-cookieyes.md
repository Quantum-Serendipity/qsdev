# GDPR Fines Statistics: Key Findings

## Largest Individual Fines

The 20 biggest GDPR fines imposed since 2018:

1. **Meta (Facebook)** — EUR 1.2 billion (May 2023)
2. **Amazon** — EUR 746 million (July 2021)
3. **Meta (Instagram)** — EUR 405 million (September 2022)
4. **Meta (Facebook & Instagram combined)** — EUR 390 million (December 2022)
5. **TikTok** — EUR 345 million (September 2023)
6. **LinkedIn** — EUR 310 million (October 2024)
7. **Uber** — EUR 290 million (2024)
8. **Facebook** — EUR 265 million (2022)
9. **Meta** — EUR 251 million (2024)
10. **WhatsApp** — EUR 225 million (September 2021)

Additional notable fines include Google (EUR 150 million), H&M (EUR 35 million), and Amazon France Logistique (EUR 32 million).

## Fine Tier Structure

The regulation establishes two penalty levels:
- Up to 2% of annual revenue or EUR 10 million, whichever is greater (lower-tier violations)
- Up to 4% of annual revenue or EUR 20 million, whichever is greater (serious breaches)

## Violation Categories

**Lower-tier violations** involve children's consent, privacy design failures, and documentation issues.

**Upper-tier violations** concern unlawful processing, transparency failures, and cross-border transfer violations.

## Notable Violation Types

- Unlawful data transfers to non-adequate jurisdictions
- Inadequate consent mechanisms for behavioral advertising
- Privacy-by-design failures affecting minors
- Employee surveillance practices
- Insufficient security measures
- Facial recognition database misuse
