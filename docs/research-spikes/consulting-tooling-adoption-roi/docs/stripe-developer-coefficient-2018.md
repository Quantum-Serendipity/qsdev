## Key Findings (from search results and secondary coverage)

- **Survey scope**: 1,000+ developers and 1,000+ C-level executives across multiple countries
- **Conducted by**: Harris Poll for Stripe, September 2018
- Developers spend **over 17 hours per week** on maintenance issues (debugging, refactoring, fixing bad code)
- About **a quarter of that maintenance time** (~4.25 hrs/week) is spent fixing bad code specifically
- The study estimates **nearly $300 billion per year** in lost global productivity due to developer time spent on maintenance vs. strategic work
- **More than 50%** of developers believe businesses can't compete because of outdated technological infrastructure
- Too much developer time allocated to **patching old systems or fixing bad code** rather than strategic work
- The report coined the term "Developer Coefficient" to describe the economic impact of developer productivity gaps
