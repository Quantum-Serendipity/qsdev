# Profitability in Consulting and Professional Services: A Simple Financial Model

- **Retrieved**: 2026-03-20

## Core Formulas

**Profitability:**
Profitability = (Revenue - Expenses) / Revenue

**Operating margin:**
Operating margin = (Gross profit - Indirect expenses) / Revenue

**Gross margin:**
Gross margin = (Revenue - Direct expenses) / Revenue

**Revenue Determination:**
Revenue = (Cost of labor + Overhead + Administrative expenses) / (1 - Target operating margin)

**Billing Rate:**
Billing Rate = Prime cost rate / (1 - Target gross margin)

## SmartSoft Case Study (10 project staff)

**Target Metrics:**
- Operating margin target: 15%
- Target gross margin: 70%
- Monthly target revenue: EUR 47,987

**Cost Structure (Monthly):**
- Prime cost of labor: EUR 14,280
- Overhead expenses: EUR 6,876
- Administrative expenses: EUR 19,633
- Total monthly costs: EUR 40,789

## Utilization & Staffing

Utilization shows what proportion of time the company managed to sell to a client. Target utilization varies by role (40-85% depending on grade/seniority).

**Prime Cost Rates (sample):**
- Senior developers: EUR 25/hour
- Mid-level developers: EUR 15/hour
- Junior developers: EUR 8/hour
- Trainees: EUR 5-6/hour

## Billing Rates (derived from 70% gross margin target)

- Senior developers: EUR 84/hour
- Mid-level developers: EUR 50/hour
- Junior developers: EUR 27/hour
- Trainees: EUR 17-20/hour

## Key Profitability Drivers (ranked)

1. Team structure and utilization planning
2. Labor cost rates
3. Target operating margin
4. Administrative overhead
