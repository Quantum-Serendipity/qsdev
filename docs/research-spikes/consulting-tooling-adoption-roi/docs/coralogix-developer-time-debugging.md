# Coralogix — Developer Time on Debugging (75% Stat)

- **Source URL**: https://coralogix.com/blog/this-is-what-your-developers-are-doing-75-of-the-time-and-this-is-the-cost-you-pay/
- **Retrieved**: 2026-03-20
- **Note**: Article does not disclose primary survey sources or methodology for its statistics. Treat as secondary/aggregated claims.

## Key Statistics Cited

- **75% of developer time** spent on debugging (approximately 1,500 hours annually)
- Developers create **70 bugs per 1,000 lines of code**
- **15 bugs per 1,000 lines of code** reach customers
- **Fixing a bug takes 30x longer** than writing a line of code
- **$113 billion spent annually** in the US on identifying and fixing product defects

## Source Quality Assessment

These statistics are widely cited but original attribution is absent in the article. The $113B figure and 75% debugging time claim appear to trace back to Cambridge University's 2013 study on software debugging costs. The "70 bugs per 1,000 LOC" claim aligns with common industry estimates but varies widely by methodology.
