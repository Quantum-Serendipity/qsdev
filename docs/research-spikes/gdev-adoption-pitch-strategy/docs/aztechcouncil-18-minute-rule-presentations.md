# Effective Sales Presentation: The 18-Minute Rule
- **Source**: https://www.aztechcouncil.org/effective-sales-presentation-the-18-minute-rule/
- **Retrieved**: 2026-05-15

## The 18-Minute Standard

TED has established 18 minutes as the ideal presentation length. According to TED curator Chris Anderson, this timeframe is "short enough to hold people's attention, including on the Internet, and precise enough to be taken seriously. But it's also long enough to say something that matters."

Carmine Gallo, author of "Talk Like TED," emphasizes that "Your ideas are your currency now... You're only as successful as your ability to communicate your ideas successfully."

## The 10-Minute Attention Threshold

Molecular biologist John Medina discusses the biological basis for attention limits in his book Brain Rules. Research indicates that audience concentration begins to decline after 10 minutes of continuous presentation material.

## Recommended Structure

To maintain engagement, Medina suggests incorporating "soft breaks" every 10 minutes. These include:
- Q&A sessions
- Video content
- Hands-on demonstrations

These interruptions help re-engage listeners and essentially reset the audience's attention clock.

## Key Takeaway

Presentations should stay below 18 minutes total and include at least one attention-renewal activity at the 10-minute mark to optimize information retention and audience engagement.
